Statistical Analysis of Behavioral Risk Factor Surveillance System Survey (BRFSS)

This project leveraged the Behavioral Risk Factor Surveillance System (BRFSS) Survey data to scrutinize disparities in physical health between males and females in the US. Through meticulous data preprocessing and in-depth exploratory analysis, including recoding, summary statistics, and visualization, a hypothesis test was conducted. The results revealed no statistically significant difference in the mean number of days of poor physical health between males and females. This investigation aims to inform healthcare interventions and policies, guiding practitioners toward more targeted and effective strategies for improving overall population health.

Analysis of Factors Involved in Climate Change Using Tableau

In this compelling data comic project, I harnessed World Bank datasets to narrate a captivating story of climate change. We embarked on a journey through key environmental indicators, including forest area, fossil fuel consumption, CO2 emissions, and renewable energy use. The project unfolded the evolving landscape of climate change, illuminating how our world has been shaped by these factors over time. It concluded with a thoughtful exploration of nations' responses to climate change, observed through their energy choices. The project not only sharpened my data cleansing and visualization skills but also fostered a deeper understanding of climate change's global impact. It delivers a clear message about the ongoing efforts and the need for further policy initiatives to combat climate change.

By merging and cleaning datasets, I extracted valuable insights into patient demographics and comorbidity correlations.
