Statistical Analysis of Behavioral Risk Factor Surveillance System Survey (BRFSS)

This project leveraged the Behavioral Risk Factor Surveillance System (BRFSS) Survey data to scrutinize disparities in physical health between males and females in the US. Through meticulous data preprocessing and in-depth exploratory analysis, including recoding, summary statistics, and visualization, a hypothesis test was conducted. The results revealed no statistically significant difference in the mean number of days of poor physical health between males and females. This investigation aims to inform healthcare interventions and policies, guiding practitioners toward more targeted and effective strategies for improving overall population health.

This project involved in-depth data preprocessing and exploratory analysis of COVID-19 patient data.
